About Me

I am an Assistant Professor at the University of Michigan's School of Information (UMSI) where I direct the STEP Collective. I study and develop socio-technical systems that support workforce development. At the moment, I am researching how technology impacts the work of small business owners, gig workers in Michigan and India, and small manufacturers.

My work contributes to the fields of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Computer Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW), social computing, and learning sciences. I have been funded by the National Science Foundation, Kauffman Foundation, UpTogether, and UM Center for Academic Innovation. Prior to my current position, I was a Presidential Postdoctoral Fellow at UMSI. I am affiliated with the University of Michigan Detroit Neighborhood Entrepreneurs Project (DNEP) and Center for Ethics, Society and Computing (ESC).

Prior to my current position, I was Presidential Post-doctoral Fellow at the University of Michigan School of Information. Prior to UMSI, I completed my PhD in Mechanical Engineering at Northwestern University's Segal Design Institute where I was funded by the NSF Graduate Research Program Fellowship. I previously interned at Microsoft and Facebook as a HCI/UX Researcher. I received my B.S. in Physics with a Minor in Mechanical Engineering from MIT in 2011.
